0 - 11 months. This age group needs MORE sleep (14-18 hours)!

For this age group, anything less than 14 hours of sleep is unhealthy.


Some Effects of not enough sleep in infants include:

Crankiness

Infants can become irritated and fussy when they don’t get enough sleep.

Poor Appetite

An infant’s ability to feed will become less effective.

Delays in Development

  • -An Infant’s cognitive (memory skills and the ability to think and reason) and motor development can be impacted negatively.
  • -An infant’s physical growth can also be impacted, as lack of sleep can cause weight gain, which can lead to obesity.
Some factors that may effect an infant's sleep include:

Hunger

Babies have tiny stomachs, so they need to eat frequently.

Discomfort

  • The following factors can cause discomfort and interrupt an infant’s sleep:
  • -Pain caused by teething
  • -Wet or dirty pampers
  • -Gassiness

Development Milestone

  • Infant's sleep may be disrupted when they are going through a development milestone, such as:
  • -Rolling over
  • -Crawling
  • -Walking
Some tips to improve an infant's sleep include:

Bedtime Routine

  • A soothing bedtime routine can include:
  • -A nice, warm bath
  • -Changing into new clothes and a fresh diaper
  • -Feeding time while cuddling
  • -Dimming the lights and giving a goodnight kiss

Naps

When an infant naps at the same time and place, it helps them sleep better at night.

Swaddle

  • -Swaddling calms and soothes the baby down to help them sleep better.
  • -It also protects the baby from their natural startle reflex which can often wake them up.

How to maintaian enough sleep in an infant:

Consistency

Stick to a consistent sleep schedule and bedtime routine to help regulate the baby's internal clock and promote better sleep patterns.

Monitoring

Monitor the baby's sleep patterns and adjust routines as needed to ensure they are getting enough restful sleep.

Addressing

Address sleep disturbances promptly to minimise their impact on overall sleep duration. In other words, respond to any disruptions in sleep, such as hunger or discomfort, immediately.
